March 17, 2026 UPDATE:

Traffic Impact Study Update,

On March 17, 2026, David Filiatreau, Traffic Engineering Manager for the City of Lexington, responded to questions regarding a Traffic Impact Study for the proposed Bryant Road development.

According to Filiatreau, a formal Traffic Impact Study is typically required only during a zoning change when a development is expected to generate more than 100 peak-hour trips. This project is not currently being reviewed as a zoning change, so a study is not required as part of this process.

However, the Planning Committee has received a Traffic Impact Review prepared by Vision Engineering, which was voluntarily submitted by the developer as an update to a previous 2018 study.

Filiatreau is currently reviewing this updated report and stated, “the counts and assumptions look fair to me.”

The city also noted that additional traffic analysis may occur in the future, as funding has been authorized to study the area for potential improvements, including the potential for a roundabout.

The full Traffic Impact Review by Vision Engineering is provided below for transparency and community awareness. Residents are encouraged to review the document and continue sharing feedback with the Planning Commission.

March 16, 2026 UPDATE:

Traffic Impact Study Requested,

Today a formal request was submitted to the Lexington-Fayette Urban County Government regarding the proposed apartment development at 1810 Bryant Rd. (Case: PLN-MJDP-26-00010).

The request asks the city to require a comprehensive Traffic Impact Study (TIS) before the project proceeds further in the development review process.

The study would evaluate potential impacts including:
  • Projected daily and peak-hour traffic generated by the development
  • Impacts to nearby intersections and roadway capacity
  • Safe ingress and egress for residents and emergency vehicles
  • Potential cut-through traffic into surrounding neighborhoods
  • Any required roadway improvements or traffic control measures

The request also asks that the study be reviewed or validated by LFUCG Traffic Engineering or an independent third-party traffic engineering firm to ensure the analysis is objective and transparent.

At this time, 20 nearby residents have formally joined the request as concerned and opposed neighbors.

What Happens Next
City planning staff will review this request as part of the ongoing development review process. If required, the developer may be asked to complete a Traffic Impact Study before the proposal can move forward.
